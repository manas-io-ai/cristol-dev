Methods to Avoid: The Scam and Waste-of-Time List

Not everything labeled "make money online" is legitimate. Here are the common traps:

  • Paid surveys and task sites. Swagbucks, InboxDollars, Amazon Mechanical Turk ‚Äî these pay $2-$5/hour. That's not income; that's exploitation. Your time is worth more than this.
  • Get-rich-quick courses. Anyone selling a course about "making $10K/month in 30 days with no experience" is making their money from course sales, not from the method they're teaching. Legitimate education exists; get-rich-quick promises don't.
  • MLM / Network marketing. Regardless of how it's branded ("social selling," "network opportunity"), if the income primarily comes from recruiting rather than selling a product to end consumers, it's a pyramid structure. FTC data shows 99% of MLM participants lose money.
  • Crypto "guaranteed returns" schemes. Staking legitimate cryptocurrencies is fine. But any platform promising guaranteed 1%/day returns, "risk-free" arbitrage, or requiring you to recruit others is a Ponzi scheme. Always.
  • Copy-paste "done for you" businesses. Someone offering to set up an e-commerce store, affiliate site, or trading bot that "runs on autopilot and generates $5K/month" is selling you a dream that doesn't exist. If it worked that well, they'd keep it themselves.
